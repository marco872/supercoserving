How Co-Serving Mortgage Platform Prospect Scheme for Low-Income Individuals Works

Co-Serving operates through the following method:
▪ Collateral Capital Participation:An interested Pension Fund will participate as collateral capital to support mortgage offers targeted at individuals with low income, based on social necessities.
▪ Return Agreement: The Pension Fund agrees to a 6% return on its investment over a commitment period of 40 years.
▪ Mortgage Coverage:The Pension Fund will cover 90% of the mortgage amount, providing essential capital for mortgage issuance.
▪ Down Payment:A Savings Account will provide the remaining 10% down payment to complete the mortgage package.
▪ Savings Account Mechanics:Borrowers will pay 1.5% interest on the amount borrowed from the Pension Fund.
▪ Profit Distribution: • Upon the sale of the property and full mortgage repayment plus interest of 6%, the profit from the capital gain will be distributed as follows: • 45% to the Pension Fund • 45% to the property owner • 10% for co-serving expenses, which includes managing the platform and creating smart contracts.
